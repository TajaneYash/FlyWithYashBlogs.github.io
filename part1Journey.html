Part I
Awakening the Spark
Journey from Enthusiast to Team Member

In the quiet of my childhood bedroom, with posters of airplanes adorning the walls and dreams of soaring through the skies, I nurtured a desire to become a pilot. Fast-forward to my university years, while amidst the rigors of engineering studies, that childhood passion found a new outlet: REMOTE CONTROLLED PLANES. As an enthusiast, I poured hours into perfecting flight techniques, tinkering with the design, and immersing myself in the world of aerodynamics. Little did I know then, that my journey would lead me to the doors of Aero Club, where I'd find a community of like-minded individuals and a platform to further pursue my passion.Today, as I stand at the helm of the Aero Club as its President, my love for RC planes burns as brightly as ever, reminding me

that no matters that my life takes me, I will forever be a hobbyist at heart, reminding me that the sky is not just a canvas for dreams but a playground where passion takes flight. In my first year of college, amid the challenges of the pandemic, I found myself immersed in the world of online lectures and virtual meets. However, a visit to campus to pay my fees during the corona period opened my eyes to different reality. I witnessed students from various club enjoying their engineering days offline, actively engaged in club activities while I remained confined to my virtual world. The contrast sparked a curiosity within me, igniting a desire to be part of the vibrant club culture on campus.

The turning point came when I stumbled upon a Google meet interview for the technical club [MITAOE AERO CLUB] on 1st Sep 2021. Unbeknownst to the seniors conducting the interview, they mistook me for a second-year student, bombarding me with questions related to subjects I had yet to encounter. It was until I clarified that I am First Year student. This situation I gracefully bowed out of the unexpected interrogation, leaving the meeting with a mixture of amusement and disbelief. As fate would have it, on 9th Sep 2021, I received an unexpected email notifying me of my selection. However, my journey as technical club member did not truly begin until three months later in January.

A call from the club captain Anuj Bhaskar, changed everything. He extended an invitation for me to join the team, offering me a place to showcase my talents and contributor to the club's endeavors. The first day proved to be a daunting challenge as I struggled to grasp complex aerodynamics concepts. Yet, during the difficulty, a passion for design emerged. Determined to excel in designing RC planes, I embarked on a journey of self-learning, immersing myself in aerodynamics principle and design techniques. And thus, my journey into the technical club began- a tale of unexpected twists, humorous mishaps, and newfound passions.

Despite the initial hurdles, I embraced the opportunity with enthusiasm, eager to learn, grow and make my mark within the club community. Now, my journey in Aero club began as a member of Team Blitzkrieg. The name itself evoked the unstoppable strategy of Hitler war machine. Our team specialized in manufacturing regular class aircrafts with wingspan exceeding 2 meters, and I was assigned to the Airframe department. My responsibilities included designing the fuselage, overseeing the landing gear, and analyzing the take-off and landing performance of RC planes. I worked alongside a talented group of individuals, each bringing their unique expertise to the table. Leading the squad was Anuj Bhaskar, whose mastery of aerodynamics, CFD and avionics guided our endeavors. Ritul Chirde served as the Vice Captain, bringing his visionary perspective to the team's direction. Pramodini Patil, hailed as the CFD expert, possed a formidable intellect capable of tackling any challenge. Pooja Khente, affectionally known as the Ironwoman, was our backbone in the structures department, while Sandeep Kulkarni brought a laid-back charm to the team while excelling in structural design. Lastly Smit Ambhore, my classmate, a fellow member with extraordinary qualities, occasionally plugged by confusion. Together, we formed a cohesive unit, blending the experience of five seniors with the fresh perspective of junior member like myself. It was within this dynamic team environment that I found my place, eager to contribute and learn from the wealth of knowledge surrounding me.

During my first technical fest at college, "Nakshatra," I stumbled upon an event called Club Mela, which I had previously been unaware of. It was a day dedicated to showcasing the achievements and projects of various clubs on campus. My captain reached out to me, informing me of a presentation task for which second-year students from the club were needed. As I navigated through the event, I realized the significance of Club Mela. Each club had its tent, proudly displaying models, achievements, and engaging in technical discussions and games. It was evident that other technical teams boasted numerous awards, while ours had none to showcase besides certificates. That day, I made a vow to myself: I would be the catalyst for a new era of award-winning achievements within our club. During Club Mela, we presented our models and even conducted virtual flying demonstrations for fellow students. It was during this event that I had the opportunity to meet alumni of the club, Vasundhara Didi and Sudhanshu Dada. Their intelligence, motivation, and willingness to help left a lasting impression on me. They offered valuable guidance in design and various other aspects, inspiring me to strive for excellence in my endeavors.

In May 2022, the time had come for the first workshop of SAE Aero Design challenge 2022 in Chennai. Excitement filled the air as Anuj, Smit, Aashish, Aditya and I prepared to represent two teams from our college. It was my inaugural experience traveling outside Maharashtra, and the prospect of a train journey added to anticipation. Booking our tickets, we found ourselves on the waiting list, hoping for a last-minute confirmation. However, luck was not on our side tickets were eventually cancelled. Determined not to miss the workshop, we embarked on the journey regardless, finding seats wherever we could on train. Arriving in Chennai, we were greeted with confusion as we navigated unfamiliar streets, searching for our hotel. The location was distant from the station, and the address provided led us astray. After much effort, we finally secured rooms late into night. The next day, we made our way to REC College for workshop. Despite the challenges, the day proved to be enlightening as we delved into basics of aerodynamics and engaged in hands on sessions building plane. Memories were made, and lessons were learned, reminding us of value in every journey, as we returned home to Pune after five eventful days, we reflected on experiences gained and resolved to be better prepared for future endeavors. Home, with its familiar comforts, welcomed me back, marking the end of a memorable journey filled with challenges and growth.

In July, excitement buzzed through our team as we received the message from SAE detailing the dates of the upcoming competition: 1st, 2nd, and 3rd September. With tickets booked, our focus shifted to manufacturing, knowing that time was of the essence. However, we faced a dilemma—we had not yet begun production. Determined to make the most of the opportunity, all seven of us drafted a letter to our college, requesting a 1.5-month holiday for dedicated manufacturing, video shooting, and flight testing. While some materials had already been purchased, others still needed to be sourced from local vendors. Stepping up to the plate, I took on the responsibility of liaising with these vendors, utilizing my bike as a means of transport. This newfound responsibility not only expanded my network within the field but also provided me with valuable insights into procurement processes. As manufacturing commenced, I quickly learned that not every problem required a technical solution—sometimes, a bit of ingenuity or "jugaad" was all that was needed. Despite the challenges, our team persevered, and soon the entire plane design was completed. The time had come for the crucial flight tests. Organizing the logistics for the flight tests was no small feat, but with careful planning and coordination, we managed to schedule a date with our pilot, Takte sir. On the day of the tests, the memory of jokes about the quaint village of "Lavale" brought a light-heartedness to the nerves that accompanied me, Soham, Ritul and Aashish to the flight ground. With bated breath, we watched as our plane took off, fearing the worst but hoping for the best. Although there were some minor setbacks during the flight round, we managed to complete it with only small failures. Returning to college, we diligently addressed these issues, conducting further flights over the next few days to ensure our plane was in optimal condition. With the flight tests completed, our focus shifted to packaging the plane for transport to Chennai. Despite the challenges of fitting everything into a limited space, we managed to pack everything into several boxes and a trunk, weighing a hefty 10 to 15 kilograms. With our preparations complete, we were ready to embark on the next chapter of our journey—off to Chennai for the competition. Stay tuned for the next installment to learn about our experiences at the competition and beyond.
